About Me

I am a Ph.D. student (2019-) at B. Thomas Golisano College of Computing and Information Sciences (GCCIS) of Rochester Institute of Technology (R.I.T), supervised by Prof. Yu Kong and co-advised by Prof. Qi Yu. I received my Master's degree (2016-2019) and Bachelor's degree (2012-2016) both at the School of Remote Sensing and Information Engineering, Wuhan University (WHU). During the years at WHU, I was advised by Prof. Daiqin Yang and Prof. Zhenzhong Chen at the Lab. of Intelligent Information Processing (IIP). My curriculum vitae is here: CV.

My goal is to achieve visual intelligence for real-world applications. My research topics are widely in scope of intelligent visual perception, recognition, and multimedia understanding. Specifically, I am experienced in human visual attention, image processing, 2D/3D object detection, video anomaly detection, and traffic accident analysis. Currently, I am interested in solving uncertainty-based decision-making problems by leveraging Bayesian deep learning, reinforcement learning, life-long learning, etc.
